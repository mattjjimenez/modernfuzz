About Modern Fuzz

Tracking Room

The studio features a large live room with tall ceilings and a spacious layout, which provide an ideal environment for tracking drums, piano, and other live acoustic instruments. The space is also equiped with a reverb chamber for adding natural ambience to recordings. At the heart of the room is a vintage inspired tube console by Tree Audio, along with an assortement of mics and effects pedals.

Experienced Producer

Mike Jimenez is a musician / song-writer / producer with 20 years of experience in the music industry. He started off in the music industry as drummer/musician before moving into production, and has worked with countless artists as an engineer, producer, and musician. He has also been fortunate to work with some of the best producers in the music industry, in some of the best recording studios. His experience ranges from tv/film/radio, to education, to worldwide touring.

Creative Environment

The studio is designed and operated to support creativity throughout the recording process. There is a separate lounge with demo station to work out new ideas or parts that need fine tuning. We also have a range of unconventional / unique instruments like concert bells, organ, mandolin, ukelele, various percussion, and a lot of effects pedals. The studio is located in a downtown within walking distance to plenty of restaurants, coffee shops, record stores, and breweries.

Recording Philosophy

At many studios, the band pays for the room and equipment, but not necessarily the production end. This is the most important part and necessary for bringing out solid performances and perfecting arrangements - ultimately translating the artist's ideas into an amazing record. I've always found this part of the creative process has always been fun, energizing, and rewarding. Perhaps growing up learning to record on 4-track cassettes is mostly responsible, but it is also due to years of working with other creative individuals, musicians, and producers. This provided invaluable music industry experience where I learned how to interact with the artist and look from their point-of-view to make the best decisions for the project. This means treating every artist/project differently, being honest about performance without infringing on creativity, and sometimes choosing an unpolished take with the best vibe - whatever is best for the record.

Mike Jimenez
